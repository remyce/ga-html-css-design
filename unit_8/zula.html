Zula

The cafe is the reincarnation of the long-standing but now defunct Pogo Cafe, with new decor and similar food. You order from a daily-changing chalkboard menu and sit at communal tables.

Herbivores will enjoy the fry-ups at weekends and pies, salads and soups during the week. The 25 teas include a panoply of rooibos and blacks, and the cappuccino with Bonsoy mylk is a winner. Kale is a regular.

Its the kind of vegan food that's heavy on animal substitutes: soy and similar takes on cheese, sausage and egg. The Monte Cristo sandwich is comforting without leaving you laden - and worth amplifying with Sriracha or tomato ketchup. The fry-up with beans, potatos, pancake, tofu scramble, mushroom and sausage (get the Vegusto for an extra pound) is more gentle than hearty, leaving room for a gingery parkin that's just sweet enough.
